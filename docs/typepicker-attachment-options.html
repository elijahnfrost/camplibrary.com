Type dropdown — three ways to attach it

Same switch-ledger TYPE row, three different ways to make the open menu feel connected to the trigger that spawned it instead of a card floating nearby. Click each TYPE control to toggle it open and closed.

All three keep the real app's colors, fonts, and the rows-below that the menu must not disturb

A. Anchored sheet

The pill unrolls: its bottom edge squares off, its border turns accent, and the menu grows straight out of it as one continuous shape — no gap, seam erased. The trigger and menu are literally joined.

C. Inline panel

The opposite bet — don't float at all. The TYPE row becomes a single framed panel: the trigger is its header, the options its body, one bordered block. It pushes the rows below down, but nothing can feel detached because it is one object.
